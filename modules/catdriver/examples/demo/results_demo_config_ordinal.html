Probability Lifts

How each driver level changes the predicted probability of the outcome compared to the reference category. Values represent percentage-point changes.

i What do probability lifts mean? catdriver / probability_lifts ▼
Probability lifts translate the odds ratios into a more intuitive metric: the percentage-point change in probability of achieving the top outcome when a driver improves by one unit. A lift of +5pp means that improving this driver by one scale point is associated with a 5 percentage-point increase in the probability of the best outcome.

Odds Ratios

Detailed coefficient table showing the odds ratio for each factor level compared to its reference category. OR > 1 means higher likelihood; OR < 1 means lower likelihood.

i Reading Odds Ratios catdriver / odds_ratios ▼
An odds ratio (OR) greater than 1 means higher scores on that driver increase the odds of a better outcome. An OR less than 1 means higher scores decrease the odds. The confidence interval shows the range of plausible values — if it crosses 1.0, the effect is not statistically significant. The forest plot visualises these intervals: markers to the right of the centre line indicate positive effects.

How to Interpret These Results

DO

  • Focus on large effects (OR > 2.0 or < 0.5) that are practically meaningful
  • Consider the ranking of drivers rather than exact OR values
  • Validate key findings with qualitative research or experiments
  • Report uncertainty ranges when presenting to stakeholders

DON'T

  • Treat odds ratios as precise population parameters
  • Make causal claims without experimental evidence
  • Over-interpret small differences (OR 1.1 vs 1.2)
  • Ignore multicollinearity or convergence warnings
Note: Odds ratios show association, not causation. With non-probability samples, p-values and confidence intervals should be treated as approximate indicators rather than strict inferential bounds.
