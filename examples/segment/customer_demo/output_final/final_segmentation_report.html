How to Interpret These Results

DO

  • Use segment profiles to understand the defining characteristics of each group
  • Validate segments with qualitative research or external data before acting on them
  • Focus on large, actionable differences between segments rather than small variations
  • Consider multiple validation metrics together (silhouette, between-SS, variable importance)
  • Re-run segmentation periodically to check if the segment structure remains stable

DON'T

  • Treat segments as fixed, immutable groups — they are statistical constructs
  • Over-interpret small sample segments (n < 30) as reliable market groups
  • Assume segment membership is binary — respondents may sit on boundaries
  • Ignore validation metrics; a poor silhouette score means the structure is unreliable
  • Use segments from non-representative samples to make population-level claims
Note: Cluster analysis finds structure in data, but the practical meaning and actionability of segments depends on domain knowledge. Always name and interpret segments in context of your research objectives.
