# CloudSync Pro — Pricing Module Demo

**Turas Pricing Module v12.0 Full Capability Showcase**

## Overview

This demo runs ALL capabilities of the Turas Pricing Module using synthetic survey data for a fictional product: **CloudSync Pro**, a cloud storage SaaS product priced in the $9-$35/month range.

Three customer segments are modelled with different willingness-to-pay:
- **Individual** (200 respondents) — price-sensitive consumers
- **Small Business** (175 respondents) — mid-range buyers
- **Enterprise** (125 respondents) — premium buyers with higher budgets

## Interpreting the Results

### Van Westendorp

The four intersection points define pricing zones:
- Below PMC: risk of quality perception issues
- PMC to OPP: acceptable but potentially underpriced
- **OPP to IDP: optimal pricing zone**
- IDP to PME: acceptable but increasing resistance
- Above PME: too expensive for most customers

### Gabor-Granger

The demand curve shows how purchase intent decreases with price. The revenue-maximising price balances higher price against lower demand. If unit cost is provided, the profit-maximising price is also computed.

### Monadic

The logistic model provides an unbiased demand curve (no anchoring effects). Key diagnostics: pseudo-R2 for model fit, price coefficient p-value for statistical significance.

### Recommendation Synthesis

When multiple methods are available, the synthesis engine combines their evidence with confidence scoring (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW) based on method agreement, sample size, and data quality.
